Stay Safe Online Checklist


1.To send money, use PINs; but do not use PIN to receive money.
2.Do not open suspicious links that come through SMS saying that your bank account or SIM card will be blocked.
3.Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) should be turned on in all of your social media and email accounts.
4.Each account should have a unique and complex passwords; use a password manager where appropriate.
5.Ensure that the operating systems on your laptop and your smartphone are up-to-date in order to address security vulnerabilities.
6.Don’t use free or unprotected Wi-Fi networks to do online banking or access sensitive portals.
7.Check the email address of the sender of the email message before downloading any attachments, regardless of whether it appears to be an email address of a professor or the university.
8.Post less about yourself on social media to avoid identity theft.
